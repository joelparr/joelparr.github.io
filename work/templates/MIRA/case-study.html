Safety, Supercars & Style. by Ian Shobbrook & Douglas MacAndrew

Opening the world's first dedicated automotive wind tunnel in 1960, MIRA has been at the forefront of research into ground vehicle aerodynamics. Since then our aerodynamicists have used our model scale and full scale wind tunnels to further understanding of vehicle aerodynamics for MIRA and for the industry as a whole.

The UK's only full-scale wind tunnel is a cost-effective solution for the aerodynamic development of a wide range of applications. Our wind tunnel is available for hire to private individuals as well as major automotive OEMs and motorsport teams.

Passive Safety Development.

The tunnel is just one of a range of complementary secure facilities all located on one site. These include a model-scale wind tunnel, climatic wind tunnels, thermofluids laboratory and our Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) modelling suite.

Complementary consultancy services are also available to support your aerodynamic development programme, while ensuring maximum value from your investment.

Although ideal for most types of vehicle this facility has been used to evaluate the aerodynamic characteristics of products as diverse as wheelie bins, skiers, life rafts, buildings and even red noses!

As an aerodynamic development tool the wind tunnels are invaluable providing detailed and accurate data in a very short amount of time. However since the 1980's MIRA's aerodynamicists have been investigating the use of computers to predict the aerodynamic performance of different vehicle shapes initially with in-house developed empirically based codes.
